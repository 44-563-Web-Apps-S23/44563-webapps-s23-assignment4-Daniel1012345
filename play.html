Daniel Omole snips a play

Title: The Gazing Globe

Author: Eugene Pillot

KATE.
I only guessed.

LADY SIMS.
Is that the usual thing?

KATE.
Oh, yes.

LADY SIMS.
They don't try to get it?

KATE.
I don't know. That is what we are told to say in the letters.

[_To her at present the only important thing about the letters is that they are ten shillings the hundred._]

LADY SIMS.

[_Returning to surer ground._]

I should explain that my husband is not a man who cares for honors. So long as he does his duty----

KATE.
Yes, I have been putting that in.
